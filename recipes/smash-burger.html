Smash Burger

Description

A burger that's made by smashing two thin beef patties into eachother with cheese in the middle.

Ingredients

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 4 (6-inch) squares of parchment paper
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 4 slices of american cheese
  • Burger toppings of choice

Steps

  1. Preheat grill and lightly oil the grate. Preheat an iron flat-top griddle until smoking.
  2. Spread butter on the indside of the buns and toast the flat-top until lightly browned. Set aside.
  3. Form meat into 8 loosely-packed balls. Do not pack tightly as this will prevent it from smashing properly.
  4. Place each ball on the flat-top griddle and cover with parchment, to prevent sticking to the spatula. Re-use parchment as needed. Smash each ball with the use of two spatulas or a small skillet.
  5. Sprinkle the meat with salt.
  6. Grill for about 45 seconds, until the edges are dark brown and the centers are a light pink color. Flip over and immediately cover 4 of them with cheese. Grill for an additional 15 to 20 seconds.
  7. Stack the plain patties over the chesse-covered ones and move each stack to a bun.
  8. Add toppings.
